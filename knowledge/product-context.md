## Product Name
Aria (Startup Ops Agent)

## One-Line Description
An AI employee that qualifies leads, onboards users, handles objections, and books meetings — autonomously, from a git repo.

## Core Pain Points Solved
List the specific problems your product solves. Aria will use these to match inbound intent.

1. Founders spend hours manually qualifying leads from their website and lose hot prospects to slow response times
2. New users drop off before experiencing the product's core value because there's no guided onboarding
3. Sales cycles stall because objections go unaddressed in async channels like email and chat
4. Early-stage startups can't afford a full-time SDR, onboarding specialist, and ops coordinator

## Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

### Who is a strong fit:
- **Role**: Founder, Head of Growth, Product Manager, Solo Developer
- **Company stage**: Pre-seed to Series A, 1–50 employees
- **Industry**: B2B SaaS, dev tools, AI/ML products, indie hacker projects
- **Signals**: Currently doing sales/onboarding manually, recently launched or in beta, has active inbound traffic (50+ visitors/day), technical enough to work with git repos

### Who is NOT a fit:
- Enterprise companies with >200 employees requiring complex procurement processes
- Agencies looking to resell under a white-label
- Non-technical users who can't work with git or CLI tools
- Companies without any inbound traffic or product-market fit signals

## Top 3 Objections You Hear Most
1. "We already have a chatbot/form tool" — Answer: This isn't a chatbot, it's a full AI employee with memory, context, and multi-skill workflows. It qualifies, onboards, and books — not just collects data.
2. "We don't have enough traffic yet" — Answer: Even 50 visitors/day is enough to see value. The agent handles each one like they're your most important customer, which matters more when volume is low.
3. "We can build this ourselves" — Answer: You can, and some teams do. But you'd need to build the qualification logic, onboarding flows, objection playbooks, meeting coordination, and keep it all in sync. This gives you that in a git repo you can fork and customize.

## Competitor Landscape
List 2–3 alternatives leads might mention. Aria won't trash them — but she'll know the difference.
1. Intercom/Drift — Great for live chat, but you're still doing the qualification manually. Aria automates the entire ops cycle.
2. Typeform/Tally — Good for collecting data, but they don't qualify, route, or book. They're forms, not agents.
3. Custom GPT/Claude Projects — Flexible but no structure, no version control, no skills system, no compliance framework.
